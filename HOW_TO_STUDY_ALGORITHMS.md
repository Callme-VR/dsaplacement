# 📖 How to Study Algorithms

Learning Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) is a marathon, not a sprint. Follow this guide to effectively master the concepts presented in this repository.

---

## 🛠️ Step 1: Master the Fundamentals

Before jumping into complex problems, ensure you understand:
- **Language Syntax**: C++ STL or Python built-in functions.
- **Big-O Notation**: Learn to calculate Time and Space complexity.
- **Basic Data Structures**: Arrays, HashMaps, Sets, and Strings.

---

## 🛤️ Step 2: Follow a Pattern-Based Approach

Don't just solve random problems. Focus on **patterns**. Study this repo in the following order:

### 1. **Arrays & Hashing** (Start Here)
- Problems like `Two Sum` and `Valid Anagram`.
- **Goal**: Understand how to store and lookup data in $O(1)$.

### 2. **Two Pointers**
- Problems like `Valid Palindrome`.
- **Goal**: Learn how to traverse arrays from both ends to reduce iterations.

### 3. **Sliding Window**
- Problems like `Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock`.
- **Goal**: Learn how to handle contiguous sub-sections of data.

### 4. **Bit Manipulation**
- Problems like `Number of 1 Bits`.
- **Goal**: Master the low-level efficiency of binary operations.

---

## 📝 Step 3: The 3-Step Problem Solving Rule

When you encounter a new problem:
1. **Try it Yourself (30 mins)**: Write down your logic on paper first. Don't look at the solution yet!
2. **Analyze the Optimized Solution**: Compare your solution with the ones in this repo. Focus on why certain choices (like using an `unordered_map`) were made.
3. **Code from Scratch**: Rewrite the code yourself without looking back at the reference.

---

## 🚀 Step 4: Consistency & Spaced Repetition

- **Re-solve**: Try a problem again after 1 week to see if you remember the core pattern.
- **Competitive Programming**: Try **Codeforces** (like the problems in `/codeforces/`) to practice solving under time constraints.
